In the final year of a course, when you are considering your final graduating portfolio and its contents, you might look at your successful projects from throughout the course (or even your less successful ones) and consider either adding to them or re-doing them to create some final portfolio works.

This is a useful exercise because you can individualise your portfolio – that is, make it distinctive from your classmates – by showing different works to them, even though the projects will be similar. It can also better demonstrate your skill sets across the portfolio.

It can also be a technique to make a potentially boring or easy assignment more interesting
