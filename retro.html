Retro futurism is a movement created before the 1960s where artist and designers tried to predict what the future would look like in the 20th century, characterized by a blend of the old fashioned retro styles with predicted futuristic technology. Retro Futurism explores the themes of tension between the past and future, and between the alienating and empowering effects of technology. Primarily reflected in artistic creations and modified technologies that realize the imagined artefacts of its parallel reality, retro-futurism has also manifested in the worlds of fashion, architecture, design, music, literature, film, and video games.

The amazing but scary thing about this movement and the creators of it was no the things they got wrong but the things they got right, plasma TVs, mobile phone, personal computers and more. Retro-futurism incorporates two overlapping trends which may be summarized as the future as seen from the past and the past as seen from the future. Retro-futurism of the first type is inevitably influenced by the scientific, technological, and social awareness of the present, and modern retro-futuristic creations are never simply copies of their pre-1960 inspirations; rather, they are given a new twist by being seen from a modern perspective.

It is directly inspired by the imagined future which existed in the minds of writers, artists, and filmmakers in the pre-1960 period who attempted to predict the future, either in serious projections of existing technology novels and stories. Such futuristic visions are refurbished and updated for the present, and offer a nostalgic, counterfactual image of what the future might have been, but is not.
